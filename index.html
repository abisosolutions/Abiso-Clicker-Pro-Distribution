✦ WHAT THE APP ACTUALLY DOES

The Windows Host opens a local WebSocket server on port 8181. It reads your open PowerPoint file and sends slide images to your phone over local WiFi. Nothing leaves your network. No cloud is involved in the presentation itself.


The Android app connects to that local server, shows slide previews, and sends navigation commands back. Firebase is used only to verify your license — not to access your files.

✦ WHY WINDOWS SHOWS A WARNING

Microsoft SmartScreen often warns the first time you run software that is new and not yet code-signed. That does not mean the file is malicious — it means Windows has not yet built reputation for this build.


Upload the EXE to VirusTotal and confirm a clean scan, then use More info → Run anyway when SmartScreen appears.

✦ WHY ANDROID BLOCKS THE APK

Android blocks APK files from outside the Play Store by default. This happens to all sideloaded apps — including major apps like Telegram which also distribute APKs directly.


Our Google Play listing is currently under process. Until it goes live, the official APK is distributed from our GitHub releases page.

Installation Guide

Step-by-Step Setup

The security warnings are expected and safe to bypass after verifying the files. Follow these steps exactly.

1
Download the Windows Host
Click the download button above. Your browser may warn the file could be harmful — click Keep or Keep anyway. This is standard for any unsigned EXE.
💡 Before proceeding, upload the EXE to virustotal.com and confirm a clean scan. Takes about 60 seconds.
2
Bypass the SmartScreen Warning
Double-click the EXE. Windows Defender SmartScreen will show "Windows protected your PC." This is expected for all new unsigned software.
⚠️ Click "More info" (the small text link below the message). A button labeled "Run anyway" will appear. Click it.
3
Complete the Installer
Follow the on-screen steps — Next, accept the license, Install. The program will be added to your Start Menu.
💡 If Windows Firewall asks to allow network access, click Allow access. This is required for your phone to connect.
4
Run as Administrator (Recommended)
Right-click the Abiso Clicker Pro shortcut → Run as administrator. This ensures smooth control of PowerPoint, especially for media-heavy presentations.
5
Open PowerPoint and Note the IP Address
Launch PowerPoint and open your file. The Abiso Host window will display your laptop's local IP address — it looks like 192.168.1.X. You will enter this into the phone app.
6
Keep the Host Window Open
Do not close the Host window during your session. You can minimize it, but closing it will disconnect your phone.
1
Download the APK to Your Phone
Open this page on your Android phone and tap "Download APK." Your browser may warn this type of file could be harmful — tap Download anyway.
💡 Alternatively, download to your PC and transfer via USB or Google Drive.
2
Allow Installation from Unknown Sources
Tap the downloaded APK file. Android will show: "Your phone is not allowed to install unknown apps from this source."
⚠️ Tap Settings in that prompt. Enable "Allow from this source" or "Install unknown apps." Press back — the install dialog resumes automatically.
3
Review Permissions and Install
Android shows the app's permissions. You will see only Internet and Camera (for QR scanning). No contacts, storage, or location access. Tap Install.
4
Play Protect Warning (if shown)
Google Play Protect may warn the app was not verified. This is standard for all sideloaded APKs. Tap "Install anyway" to continue.
💡 For maximum assurance, scan the APK with Malwarebytes Mobile before this step.
5
Connect and Start Presenting
Open Abiso Clicker Pro. Ensure your phone and laptop are on the same WiFi. Enter the IP address shown in the Host window, or tap the QR icon to scan the code. Tap CONNECT.
6
Disable "Unknown Sources" After Install
Go to Settings → Apps → Special App Access → Install unknown apps and turn off the permission you granted in Step 2. The app is installed — you no longer need it enabled.
FAQ

Common Questions

Answers to the most common concerns before downloading.

Is this a virus? My antivirus flagged it.+
No. Both files are clean. Antivirus programs sometimes flag unsigned software with a "false positive" — they don't recognize the new file, not that they found malicious code. Upload the file to VirusTotal.com to check it against 70+ engines. A clean result there is far more reliable than a single antivirus flagging a new file.
Why isn't the app on the Google Play Store?+
Our Play Store listing is currently under process. As a small independent team in the Philippines, we will publish there as soon as the listing is approved. In the meantime, the APK is distributed from our official GitHub releases page.
Does the app access my PowerPoint files or send them to the internet?+
No. Your files never leave your local network. The Windows Host reads your open PowerPoint file, converts slides to images, and sends them directly to your phone over local WiFi — never to any cloud server. The only internet traffic is the license check with Firebase when you sign in.
My phone and laptop can't connect. What do I do?+
Both devices must be on the same WiFi network. If your office or school WiFi blocks device-to-device connections, enable Mobile Hotspot on your Windows laptop and connect your phone's WiFi to it. This creates a private direct link that bypasses network restrictions entirely.
What happens to my Pro license if I lose my phone?+
Your license is tied to your Google account, not your device. Get a new phone, download the APK, sign in with the same Google account, and your Pro status is restored automatically — no support ticket, no extra payment.
Does it work without internet during my presentation?+
Yes. Once signed in at least once with internet, your Pro license is cached to your phone's local storage. After that, no internet is needed to confirm your license. The presentation control runs entirely over local WiFi between phone and laptop.
What are the system requirements?+
Windows Host: Windows 10 or later, Microsoft PowerPoint (any recent version), .NET 8 Runtime (installer will prompt if absent). 4 GB RAM recommended.

Android App: Android 8.0 (Oreo) or later. Any Android phone made after 2018 will work fine.
